What is SDG 2?

Zero Hunger – Our Mission

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 2 aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030.

🛡️ Food Security

Ensuring all people have access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food throughout the year.

🥗 Nutrition

Ending all forms of malnutrition including stunting, wasting and obesity in children and mothers.

🌱 Sustainable Agriculture

Doubling agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers sustainably.

🤝 Community Support

Building resilient food systems that support local communities and reduce vulnerability.

828 million people go to bed hungry every night worldwide.

1.3 billion tons of food is wasted globally every year.

45% of child deaths are linked to malnutrition globally.

Indian Food Security Schemes

Explore major government initiatives working towards hunger-free and nutritionally secure India.

🍚

Public Distribution System (PDS)

Provides essential food grains like rice, wheat, and sugar at subsidized rates to low-income families.

👨‍👩‍👧 80+ Crore Beneficiaries
🥗

Mid-Day Meal Scheme

Nutritious meals provided to school children to improve attendance, nutrition, and learning outcomes.

🏫 Covers Government Schools
👶

Integrated Child Development Services

Supports children under 6 years and pregnant mothers through nutrition, immunization, and health services.

❤️ Mother & Child Care
🌾

National Food Security Act

Legal framework ensuring affordable food grains and nutritional security for vulnerable populations.

The Hidden Crisis

Hunger & Food Waste Are Connected

While millions sleep hungry every night, huge amounts of food are wasted daily. Reducing waste can directly fight hunger.

🍱 1.3 Billion Tons Wasted

Every year, enough food to feed billions is thrown away globally.

😔 828 Million Hungry People

Millions still struggle daily to access nutritious food and clean meals.

🌍 Environmental Impact

Food waste increases greenhouse gas emissions and wastes water, land, and energy resources.

🤝 Small Actions Matter

Meal planning, donating leftovers, and proper storage can reduce hunger significantly.
